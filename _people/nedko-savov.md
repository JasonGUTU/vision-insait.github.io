**About me**

I am Nedko Savov, a PhD student at the [Institute for Computer Science, Artificial Intelligence and Technology](https://insait.ai/), [Sofia University](https://www.uni-sofia.bg/). I am supervised by Prof. [Luc Van Gool](https://insait.ai/prof-luc-van-gool/).

I am broadly interested in teaching computers to understand and generate visual worlds – from videos to interactive environments.

My research focuses on advancing **interactive world models** – generative systems that learn to **simulate and create interactive visual worlds**. My work explores **vision-based generative modeling** with an emphasis on **interaction, long context processing, memory abilities, and generalization** across many environments.

I am also interested in **self-supervised learning, latent action representations, controllable video and image editing, and efficient video generation. **Through my past work, I also have a background in face analysis and synthesis, 3D scenes, computational photography.
